**Tool Name:** Learning Generative AI Tools for Excel (O'Reilly Early Release)

## Summary

A practical guide to integrating AI assistants — including Microsoft Copilot, ChatGPT, and other generative AI tools — into everyday Excel workflows. The book covers the full spectrum from basic prompt engineering for spreadsheets to advanced techniques for data cleaning, formula generation, pivot table automation, and chart creation.

## What It Covers

| Chapter Area | What You'll Learn |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **Getting Started** | Setting up Copilot and ChatGPT for Excel, understanding what AI can (and can't) do in spreadsheets |
| **Data Preparation** | Using AI to clean messy data, handle missing values, standardise formats, and merge datasets |
| **Formulas & Functions** | Generating complex formulas with natural language prompts, debugging formula errors with AI |
| **Analysis** | AI-assisted pivot tables, trend detection, and summary statistics |
| **Visualisation** | Creating charts and dashboards with AI guidance, choosing the right chart type |
| **Advanced Uses** | Automating repetitive workflows, connecting Excel to external AI APIs |

## Who Should Read This

- **Researchers** who use Excel for initial data exploration before moving to R or Python
- **M&E practitioners** managing programme data in spreadsheets
- **Analysts** looking to speed up repetitive data preparation tasks
- **Anyone** who spends hours on manual Excel work that could be partially automated

## Why It Matters

Researchers and development practitioners often use Excel as their primary data tool — for budgets, survey data, indicator tracking, and reporting. Integrating AI into these workflows can:

- **Save time** on repetitive tasks like data cleaning and formula writing
- **Reduce errors** by letting AI check formula logic and flag inconsistencies
- **Lower the barrier** for non-programmers to do more sophisticated analysis
- **Free up time** for deeper analytical work that AI can't do

## Limitations to Keep in Mind

- AI-generated formulas should always be verified — they can be confidently wrong
- Sensitive data (beneficiary information, health records) should not be sent to external AI services
- AI tools work best as assistants, not replacements, for analytical thinking
