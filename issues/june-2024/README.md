## Featured Items

### 1. State of Food Security and Nutrition 2023 (SOFI Report)

The annual FAO/WHO/UNICEF/WFP/IFAD flagship report on global hunger. The 2023 edition finds that approximately 735 million people faced hunger in 2022 — up from 613 million pre-pandemic. The report emphasises urbanisation as a transformative force for food systems and calls for integrated urban-rural food policies.

**Key takeaway:** Food insecurity is not just a rural problem. Urban food systems require dedicated policy attention, especially in rapidly urbanising regions of sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia.

### 2. IPCC Synthesis Report

The capstone of the IPCC's Sixth Assessment cycle, summarising findings across all three working groups. Confirms that global temperatures have already risen 1.1°C above pre-industrial levels and that limiting warming to 1.5°C requires emissions to peak before 2025 and fall 43% by 2030.

**Key takeaway:** The window for action is narrowing rapidly. Every fraction of a degree matters, and adaptation investment in vulnerable communities is critically underfunded.

### 3. Gender Equality in the Ocean Economy

A joint UN Women and UN Global Compact report examining the barriers women face in marine industries — from fisheries to maritime transport to coastal tourism. Documents both the exclusion of women from ocean governance and the economic cost of that exclusion.

**Key takeaway:** Gender-blind ocean policy leaves economic value on the table. The report provides a framework for gender-responsive blue economy strategies.

### 4. Decolonising Research Methods

A collection of resources challenging the dominance of Western epistemologies in global research. Covers participatory approaches, indigenous knowledge systems, and practical steps for shifting power in research partnerships.

**Key takeaway:** Decolonising research is not just an academic debate — it affects what questions get asked, whose data counts, and which communities benefit from research outputs.

### 5. OpenAlex and ROR

Two open-access tools for scholarly metadata and institutional mapping:

- **OpenAlex** — a free, open catalogue of the world's scholarly works, authors, and institutions (a successor to Microsoft Academic Graph)
- **ROR (Research Organization Registry)** — a community-led registry of persistent identifiers for research organisations

**Key takeaway:** These tools make it possible to map research networks, track funding flows, and identify collaboration patterns without expensive proprietary databases.
